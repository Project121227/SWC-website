What is Osteopathy?

Osteopathy is a hands-on, drug-free, and holistic approach to healthcare that views the body as a single, integrated unit rather than a collection of separate parts. It focuses on identifying and treating the root cause of pain and dysfunction, rather than just addressing symptoms.

This approach is patient-centred — meaning every treatment is tailored to the individual's unique condition, lifestyle, and needs.

Our Approach

Osteopathic treatment is gentle, precise, and scientifically based, using manual techniques to improve mobility, enhance circulation, and support the body's natural healing mechanisms.

By restoring balance within the body, osteopathy helps improve overall function and well-being.

Osteopathy in India

Osteopathy is a well-established system of healthcare globally and has been steadily gaining recognition in India over the past decade.

In India, its growth has been supported by initiatives inspired by Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar, founder of the Art of Living Foundation, with a vision to bring holistic and integrative healthcare approaches to the country.

One of the leading institutions offering formal education in osteopathy is Sri Sri University, located in Bhubaneswar. The university offers structured programs such as BSc, MSc, and Integrated MSc in Osteopathy, contributing to the development of professionally trained osteopaths in India.

Our Presence in Andhra Pradesh

Osteopathy is still an emerging field in India, with limited availability of qualified practitioners.

At Sattvaa Wellness Center, we are proud to be among the early practitioners bringing osteopathy to Andhra Pradesh — offering authentic, patient-centered osteopathic care.

Our mission is to make this gentle, scientific, and holistic approach accessible to more people, helping them experience true healing beyond symptom-based treatment.

Scientific Foundation

Osteopathy is grounded in anatomy, physiology, and biomechanics, with growing scientific research supporting its effectiveness in improving pain, mobility, and overall quality of life.

It is recognized globally, including by the World Health Organization, which provides benchmarks and guidelines for osteopathic training and practice.
